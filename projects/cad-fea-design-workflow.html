CAD and FEA Mechanical Design Workflow

From concept geometry to simulation-backed design decisions

Description

This page summarizes my CAD/FEA/prototyping work across multiple mechanical projects, from aerospace tooling and mobility systems to thermodynamic components and biomechanical analysis.

The workflow spans full-lifecycle engineering: concept and requirements, kinematic/dynamic modeling, CAD implementation, FEA/simulation validation, and prototype iteration with both FDM and advanced powder-bed workflows such as HP Jet Fusion 4200.

Representative Engineering Cases

  • Splicers (Airbond): Turned aerospace product concepts into functioning prototypes using SolidWorks, PLC collaboration, and rapid 3D-printing loops; documented best-practice workflows to improve time/cost efficiency.
  • Off-road Wheelchair: Led part design and structural/kinematic analysis for a 3-wheel concept (120kg user load, 24kg wheelchair, aluminum/carbon-fiber design) with bicycle-principle hand levers for torque-assisted propulsion.
  • Piston Ring: Modeled piston skirt-ring-wall system and derived thermodynamic/dynamic constraints; achieved design targets including 188000-cycle life expectancy and optimized ring geometry/tolerances.
  • Scroll Expander (ORC): Designed geometry and thermodynamic model for a 1.5kW ORC case (3000 rpm, 11bar/1.5bar, 130C inlet), then optimized dimensions to reach 92% volumetric efficiency.
  • Femur Bone FEA: Performed CT-based segmentation/surface prep and ANSYS evaluation across standing and jump-load scenarios (70-180kg), identifying force-distribution behavior under multiple conditions.

Capabilities Demonstrated
