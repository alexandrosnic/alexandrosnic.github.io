End-to-End Deep Learning for Autonomous Driving

Teaching vehicles human-like intuition through Behavioral Cloning and CNNs

The Challenge

Classical robotics relies on hard-coded rules: "If Lidar detects X, then steer Y." However, real-world driving contains infinite edge cases that rules cannot cover. The goal of this project was to implement Behavioral Cloning—a technique where a vehicle learns to drive by mimicking human steering patterns directly from raw visual data.

My Contribution: From Perception to Control

I developed a multi-stage deep learning pipeline. First, I engineered a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for traffic sign recognition, achieving high accuracy in classifying stop signs, speed limits, and warnings under varying lighting conditions.

The core of the project involved training a deep neural network to map raw pixels from a front-facing camera directly to steering commands. By utilizing Data Augmentation (flipping images, adjusting brightness) and a customized NVIDIA-style CNN architecture, I trained the model to predict the steering angle with micro-precision. The result was an agent that could navigate complex, winding tracks in simulation entirely through "intuition" learned from human driving logs.

Technical Deep Dive

  • Architecture: Implemented a deep CNN using Keras/TensorFlow with dropout layers to prevent overfitting and ensure generalization to new tracks.
  • Perception: Leveraged OpenCV for image preprocessing, including YUV color space conversion and normalization to stabilize gradient descent.
  • Behavioral Cloning: Integrated human-driven training datasets to teach the model recovery maneuvers (how to steer back to the center if it drifts).
  • Transfer Learning: Explored the application of pre-trained models for feature extraction in unstructured driving environments.
