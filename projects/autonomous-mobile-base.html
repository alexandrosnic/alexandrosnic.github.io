Full-Stack Autonomous Navigation & Robotic Deployment

From solo R&D to international field deployment in Singapore

The Challenge

Construction sites are among the most difficult environments for robotics: they are dynamic, unstructured, dusty, and constantly changing. As the first employee at Conbotics, I was tasked with taking a concept for an autonomous wall-painting robot and turning it into a market-ready mobile platform capable of precise navigation in these harsh conditions.

The Build: Solo Navigation Stack

I took 100% ownership of the software architecture for the mobile base. This involved implementing a robust Navigation Stack from the ground up using ROS. I integrated SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) for environment discovery and utilized EKF-based Sensor Fusion to merge data from Lidar, IMU, and encoders for pinpoint localization. To handle site-specific obstacles, I engineered a perception layer using YOLOv4 and Realsense depth cameras for real-time semantic mapping.

Beyond first-pass integration, I tuned the full autonomy chain end-to-end: multi-sensor data acquisition, feature extraction, odometry filtering, and local/global planning behavior under real construction-site uncertainty. I evaluated both EKF and UKF fusion strategies and iterated planner/odometry parameters to improve corner detection, stable wall-following, and reliable wall-changing maneuvers.

This required hands-on hardware integration of LiDAR, depth cameras, IMUs, and embedded compute interfaces, including field tuning of sensor synchronization and communication paths so perception and control stayed stable under dust, vibration, and varying illumination.

The Test: Singapore Field Deployment

In 2025, I transitioned from R&D to Lead Field Deployment Engineer for a mission-critical pilot in Singapore. This was where I bridged the "Sim-to-Real" gap. On-site, I identified discrepancies between our Gazebo simulations and the physical constraints of the construction site.

By performing deep Rosbag and telemetry analysis in the field, I optimized the path-planning parameters and PID controllers to handle varying floor frictions and lighting conditions. This direct intervention helped the company reach market readiness 6 months earlier than projected.

Technical Deep Dive

  • Navigation: Developed custom global and local planners for precise wall-following and obstacle avoidance.
  • Localization & Fusion: Tuned odometry and sensor-fusion pipelines (EKF/UKF candidates) for drift-robust localization in dusty, dynamic scenes.
  • Sim-to-Real: Synchronized simulation environments with real-world sensor noise and physical constraints.
  • Hardware-in-the-loop: Optimized CAN Bus communication and motor driver feedback loops for smooth motion.
  • Field Engineering: Conducted stress-tests and on-site debugging in unstructured environments, ensuring 99% mission uptime.
