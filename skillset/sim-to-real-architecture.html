Sim-to-Real Architecture

Description

I build robots that don't know the difference between a simulation and the real world. I develop unified architectures where the transition from a Gazebo or Webots world to a physical robot is handled by a single command. I work extensively with URDF, STL, and CAD (SolidWorks) to build high-fidelity digital twins. To bridge the 'Sim2Real' gap, I utilize Docker and Bash-parameterized launch files that dynamically load specific tuning parameters depending on the environment, ensuring the software stack remains identical whether running on a local GPU or an embedded robot controller.

Key Achievement

Reduced deployment time by creating a 'one-command' launch system that automatically handles environment-specific parameters for both industrial arms and mobile bases. I developed such solutions for both Conbotics and the University of Potsdam.
