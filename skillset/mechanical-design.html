Mechanical Design (CAD), FEA & Advanced Prototyping

Description

I approach robotics from the ground up, starting with the mechanical integrity of the system. My expertise in CAD and Finite Element Analysis (FEA) allows me to bridge the gap between abstract physics and functioning hardware. I use simulation to fail fast and iterate quickly. I am proficient in the entire design lifecycle—from kinematic and dynamic analysis in Matlab/Simulink to high-fidelity modeling in SolidWorks and advanced manufacturing through 3D printing (FDM & Industrial HP Jet Fusion). Some of the projects I've worked on include: * Aerospace R&D & Prototyping (Airbond): I translated conceptual aerospace product ideas into functioning prototypes for composite material splicers. By combining SolidWorks design with PLC-driven electronics, I reduced the 'idea-to-prototype' cycle. I also established a 'Best Practices' framework for the company to optimize time efficiency and profit margins for products entering a multi-million-pound market. * Human-Centric Design & Kinematics: For the off-road wheelchair project, I led the mechanical design and ergonomics analysis. The challenge was creating a robust, 24kg aluminium and carbon-fiber frame capable of handling 120kg loads in rugged environments. My design used bicycle-principle hand levers to maximize driving torque, a project that is still used today at my university as a gold-standard example for new students. * Thermodynamic Modeling & Optimization: I engineered a scroll expander for an Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC) system. This involved complex calculations of combustion chamber volumes, mass/energy equilibrium, and flow pulses. By modeling the system in Simulink and optimizing the geometry in ANSYS, I achieved a volumetric efficiency of 92%. * Biomechanical FEA: Using CT scan data, I performed a full segmentation and smoothing of a human femur to conduct FEA. I simulated diverse loading conditions—ranging from static standing to high-impact landing scenarios for weights up to 180kg—to identify force distribution patterns. This project required a deep understanding of mesh optimization and material property assignment in ANSYS.
