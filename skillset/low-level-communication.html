Low-level systems communication

Description

Robotics is often a battle against latency. At Bosch, I went deep into the 'plumbing' of autonomous stacks to ensure sensors could talk to CPUs/GPUs without the overhead that usually kills real-time performance. I co-developed and unit-tested shared memory solutions for CUDA (C++) to maximize throughput and minimize latency. One of the biggest wins was identifying and fixing a bug within the Linux kernel itself while working with the SMC-R library for RDMA communication. It’s one thing to write code; it’s another to fix the OS it runs on.

Key Achievement

Analyzed multi-PC communication bottlenecks using Wireshark and eBPF; researched RDMA vs. Zenoh for next-gen ROS2 architectures.
