Autonomous Navigation Stack Development

Description

I develop full-stack navigation solutions that take a robot from 'blind' to 'autonomous.' This includes everything from data acquisition (merging multi-sensor inputs), sensor fusion (EKF/UKF) and feature extraction, to local and global path planning and task planning. I focus on tuning SLAM, odometry and planners to ensure smooth, collision-free movement in dynamic, challenging environments. The autonomous navigation field fascinated me early on in my career. My bachelor thesis on the interaction between human- and autonomous-driven vehicles, was selected for the Cyprus Ministry of Education first prize award for best student project and NTUA’s second prize award for the most innovative project.

Key Achievement

Worked with multiple autonomous mobile bases, but most importantly, I built and tuned a complete navigation stack for a construction robot that transitioned from a Gazebo prototype to a physical mobile base capable of corner detection, autonomous wall-following and wall changing.
